Google I/O 2025: Google Beam

Revolutionizing 3D Video Calls

Formerly known as Project Starline, Google Beam is a groundbreaking 3D video communication platform unveiled at Google I/O 2025. Its core purpose is to create the profound sensation of being in the same room as remote participants, transforming virtual interactions into a more natural and human experience.

This technology has evolved beyond a research project and is now being prepared for commercial deployment, with strategic partnerships with industry leaders like HP and Zoom.

Google Beam leverages a sophisticated combination of AI, advanced 3D imaging, and specialized light field displays. It captures standard 2D video streams and transforms them into realistic, high-fidelity 3D representations of participants. These representations are then rendered life-size, allowing for a sense of physical presence without the need for cumbersome headsets or glasses.

The system is built on the power and scale of Google Cloud, ensuring enterprise-grade reliability and seamless integration with existing workflows.
